Topic 9 — Fine-Tuning

Summary: Fine-tuning adapts a pre-trained model to a specific task by continuing training on your data — making the generalist an expert at your use case while retaining broad capabilities.

Why It Exists

  • Problem — pre-trained models are generalists. They don't know your company's jargon, don't follow your output format, and may not perform well on niche domains.
  • Solution — continue training on YOUR data (100–10,000 examples) so the model learns YOUR patterns. Much cheaper than pre-training from scratch.
  • Mental Model — On-the-job training for an experienced hire. You hired a senior dev (pre-trained model) who knows Java/Python broadly. A few weeks of onboarding (fine-tuning) teaches them YOUR codebase and conventions without re-learning programming.
  • Or: Like extending a base class. The base class (pre-trained weights) provides general functionality; your subclass (fine-tuning) overrides specific behaviors for your domain.

How It Works

Choose base model → prepare data → configure training → train → evaluate → deploy.

Fine-tuning pipeline: pre-trained base + your task data → LoRA training → domain-specific model

LoRA — How It Works

LoRA: freeze original weights W, train tiny adapter matrices A×B. 0.04% params trained, ~same quality.

Decision heuristic: Try prompting first. If it fails consistently on format/style/domain, fine-tune. Fine-tuning is the "next step" when prompting hits its ceiling — like moving from config to code when config isn't expressive enough.

Common Misconceptions

WrongCorrect
"Fine-tuning = training from scratch"Starts from pre-trained weights. Much cheaper ($10s vs $millions).
"Need millions of examples"100–1000 quality examples often sufficient for LoRA.
"Fine-tuning always beats prompting"For simple tasks, a good prompt is enough. Fine-tune when prompting fails.
"Fine-tuned model keeps all base knowledge"Catastrophic forgetting is real — it can LOSE base capabilities. Balance matters.
"LoRA is inferior to full fine-tuning"In practice, LoRA with r=16-64 matches full fine-tuning for most tasks.
