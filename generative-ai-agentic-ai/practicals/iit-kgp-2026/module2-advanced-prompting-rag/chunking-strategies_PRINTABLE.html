Module 2, Topic 2 — Chunking Strategies

Chunking is how you split documents into pieces before embedding them. Each chunk becomes one vector in your store — and one potential retrieval result. Too large and retrieval is noisy; too small and context is incomplete. The chunking strategy directly determines what the LLM sees as "context," making it the foundation layer of RAG quality. Module 1 introduced chunking as a concept; here you're choosing the right strategy for your content type and tuning it for optimal retrieval.

Problem: Poor chunking → irrelevant retrieval → bad answers. Chunking is the foundation of RAG quality — get it wrong and everything downstream suffers regardless of embedding model or vector store.
